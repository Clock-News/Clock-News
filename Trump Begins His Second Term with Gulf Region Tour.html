Trump Begins His Second Term with Gulf Region Tour

Three-Day Trip Across the Middle East

President Donald Trump has started his first international trip of his second term by visiting three important countries in the Gulf region: Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates (UAE).

Trip Schedule

  • Monday: Leaves the United States
  • Tuesday: Arrives in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia
  • Wednesday: Attends a Gulf summit and travels to Qatar
  • Thursday: Finishes his tour in the UAE

Main Goals of the Visit

Trump’s trip focuses on boosting the economy and improving diplomatic ties. Key issues include:

  • A possible ceasefire in Gaza
  • Progress on peace talks between Saudi Arabia and Israel
  • Greater security cooperation among Gulf countries

He may also announce whether the US will continue calling the sea between Iran and the Arabian Peninsula the "Persian Gulf" or change it to the "Arabian Gulf".

Saudi Arabia: Business Deals and Peace Efforts

Trump is encouraging Saudi Arabia to join the Abraham Accords, which helped normalize Israel's relations with the UAE and Bahrain during his first term. Talks were delayed due to the Gaza conflict, but Trump might present a new peace plan.

Unlike President Biden, Trump is not linking Saudi Arabia’s nuclear energy plans to peace with Israel. Saudi Arabia is asking for US support to build a peaceful nuclear program.

Trump also wants to lock in a $1 trillion investment from Saudi Arabia in US businesses, adding to the $600 billion already pledged by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman.

Saudi Arabia is also expected to announce over $100 billion in weapons purchases, including:

  • Missiles
  • Radar equipment
  • Transport planes

Talks may restart on updating the US-Saudi defense agreement.

UAE: Technology and AI Collaboration

Trump will meet UAE President Mohammed bin Zayed to discuss a huge $1.4 trillion investment plan focused on:

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI)
  • Semiconductors
  • Energy
  • Manufacturing

Trump is also expected to lift restrictions placed during Biden’s term on high-tech US exports to the UAE. This will help the UAE achieve its goal of becoming a global leader in AI by 2031.

Qatar: Military and Middle East Conflicts

In Qatar, Trump will meet Emir Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani. They will talk about:

  • Strengthening military cooperation (Qatar hosts the largest US base in the region)
  • Improving regional security coordination

Qatar may ask the US to ease sanctions on Syria and will likely bring up the ongoing Gaza ceasefire talks, as Qatar plays a key mediating role in the region.

Why Trump Isn’t Visiting Israel

Due to the rising violence in Gaza and tensions with Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu, Trump has decided not to visit Israel on this trip.

Reports say there is growing mistrust between Trump and Netanyahu, and Trump is aiming for a more independent approach to Middle East policy.

Reaction from the Region

All three countries — Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE — have promised major investments in the US ahead of Trump’s visit, showing signs of strong cooperation.

However, Trump’s earlier idea of moving Gaza residents to other Arab countries received strong criticism. Egypt, Qatar, UAE, and Kuwait rejected it, saying they support the right of Palestinians to stay in their own land.
