About Clock News

Clock News started as a small project by someone who wasn’t a professional journalist or developer — just someone who cared about real news and wanted to share it clearly and honestly. There's no team or funding behind it, and everything is built step by step, learning as it goes.

The goal is simple: give people a place where they can read news without the drama. No clickbait, no noise — just meaningful updates about politics, sports, technology, and the world, with special attention to South Asia.

Clock News uses only trusted sources like BBC News, Al Jazeera English, Reuters, Dawn, The Hindu, The Daily Star, The Kathmandu Post, The Japan Times, The Straits Times, South China Morning Post, TechCrunch, Nature News, and Muslim News. While updates may not always be immediate, they aim to be real — not rushed, not hyped.

This site is also part of a learning journey. It's built by someone who's still learning coding, design, and writing — combining small skills to build something bigger over time. It’s not about perfection. It’s about progress.

Clock News hopes to grow into something more useful — maybe even inspiring others who are starting with little but want to build something honest.

Truly appreciate your visit. If even one thought here made you pause, reflect, or see things a little more clearly, then this effort has been worthwhile.
