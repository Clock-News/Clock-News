Over 100 Killed in Gaza as Palestinians Mark 77 Years Since the Nakba

By Subworldnews | May 15, 2025 | AL JAZEERA AND NEWS AGENCIES

More than 100 Palestinians, including many women and children, were
killed in new Israeli airstrikes across Gaza. The attacks happened
as Palestinians marked the 77th anniversary of the Nakba – the mass
displacement of over 750,000 Palestinians during the creation of Israel
in 1948.


At least 59 people died overnight in Khan Younis, a city
in southern Gaza. Local doctors say most of the victims
were children and families. In the north, airstrikes also
hit Gaza City and Jabalia.


Al Jazeera’s reporter, Tareq Abu Azzoum, called it
“another bloody day” and said Israeli jets hit nine
homes in Khan Younis without warning. Many families were
completely wiped out. He said the attacks seemed to
target civilians, not military sites. In Jabalia, 13
raids hit the refugee camp and nearby areas while
people were sleeping.


Rescue teams are overwhelmed and short on equipment
to help those trapped under rubble. Abu Azzoum said
the bombings are tearing apart Gaza’s social fabric,
not just buildings.


Thousands Flee Their Homes


Following the attacks, thousands of people were forced
to flee Gaza City after the Israeli army gave sudden
evacuation orders. Al Jazeera’s Hani Mahmoud reported
scenes of chaos, with families rushing to escape,
carrying what little they could.


“There’s no safe place,” he said. “Even shelters
have been bombed.”


One displaced man, Hasan Moqbel, called the situation
a war on civilians. “They’ve been bombing Gaza for
19 months. There are no fighters here – just children
and old people dying,” he said.


People in Gaza are especially fearful on Nakba Day,
worried that Israel may launch a bigger ground
attack. “They’re scared of being forced to flee again,
to even worse conditions,” said Abu Azzoum.


Despite international efforts to stop the violence,
the attacks are only increasing.


Qatar Calls for Ceasefire – Trump Suggests U.S. Control Gaza


In Qatar, Emir Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani urged
U.S. President Donald Trump to help push for a
ceasefire.


“Our diplomacy is focused on stopping the bloodshed
and freeing hostages,” he said. He warned that the war
is threatening stability in the whole region – from
the West Bank to Yemen and Lebanon.


Trump responded by sharing his own idea: for the U.S.
to take control of Gaza and turn it into a “freedom zone.”
He said he would be proud if the U.S. led the rebuilding.


Meanwhile, U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff told Al Jazeera
he felt hopeful about reaching a deal for Gaza that
could include food, medical aid, and possibly a ceasefire.


Israeli Minister Calls for West Bank Assault


In the West Bank, things are also getting worse.
Israel’s far-right Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich
called for destroying Palestinian towns, just like what’s
happening in Gaza.


“Just like we are flattening Gaza, we need to
flatten terror hubs in the West Bank,” he said,
pointing to the village of Bruqin.


On Thursday morning, Israeli forces raided several
cities and refugee camps across the West Bank –
including Nablus, Bethlehem, and others. People reported
arrests, home invasions, and mistreatment by soldiers.


With both Gaza and the West Bank under attack,
many Palestinians are losing hope that any part
of their homeland will be left untouched.
